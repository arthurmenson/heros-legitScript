What is erectile dysfunction (ED)?

ED is the inability to get or maintain an erection firm enough for sex. It's common, affecting over 30 million men in the US. Causes include stress, health conditions, medications, or lifestyle factors.

What ED medications do you prescribe?

We prescribe FDA‑approved medications including sildenafil (generic Viagra), tadalafil (generic Cialis), and vardenafil (generic Levitra). Your provider will recommend the best option based on your health and preferences.

How long do ED medications take to work?

Most ED medications work within 20–60 minutes. Sildenafil and vardenafil last 4–6 hours, while tadalafil can last up to 36 hours. Take on an empty stomach for faster results.

Are there side effects with ED medication?

Common mild side effects include headache, flushing, or stuffy nose. Most men tolerate ED medications well. Your provider will review your health history to minimize risks and ensure safety.
